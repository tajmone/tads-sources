Details

An "except" noun phrase is a normal "complete" noun phrase or a possessive noun phrase that doesn't explicitly qualify another phrase (for example, "all the coins but bob's" - the "bob's" is just a possessive noun phrase without another noun phrase attached, since it implicitly qualifies "the coins").
